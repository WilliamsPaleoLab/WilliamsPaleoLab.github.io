Dr. Quinn Asena, former postdoc of the Williams Lab, has an exciting new paper out in [Methods in Ecology and Evolution](https://besjournals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/2041-210x.70315), which presents a new statistical model for testing hypotheses about the environmental and biotic drivers of community dynamics.  The model, called multinomialTS, is a state-space model that is specifically designed to work with community time series that are irregularly sampled over time, and with community count data that have a sum-to-100% constraint.  MultinomialTS can be used with any kind of community time series data  (e.g. repeated community censuses from plots), but is particularly well suited to work with count-based paleoecological datasets such as for pollen, foraminfera, and other micropaleontological data.  Co-author Dr. Tony Ives was instrumental to developing the mathematical backbone of multinomialTS, and test datasets from Sunfish Pond, PA were provided by co-authors Jonathan Johnson, Bryan Shuman, and Vania Stefanova.  The paper is accompanied by an R package available from Quinn's [GitHub repo](https://github.com/QuinnAsena/multinomialTS) and a how-to vignette.  Good work Quinn and congratulations!
